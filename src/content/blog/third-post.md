There's something about instrumental music that makes study hours feel different. I don't know if it's science or magic — probably a little of both. What I do know is that since I found my ideal playlist, my Korean study sessions are longer and less draining.

Here's what I listen to and why it works.

## Why Instrumental and Not K-pop

I know. It sounds contradictory that someone studying Korean doesn't study with K-pop. But there's a problem: when I listen to lyrics in the language I'm learning, part of my brain goes off to process what they're saying instead of focusing on what I'm reading.

Instrumental music avoids that. Your brain gets just enough musical stimulation to stay engaged, without losing focus.

There's one exception: when I'm doing vocabulary flashcards, I sometimes put K-pop on in the background. The listening context actually helps anchor new words.

## What's in My Playlist

**Korean lo-fi.** There are entire YouTube channels with lo-fi beats and a Korean aesthetic — Seoul cafés, rain on windows, Korean animation in the background. They're perfect. Search "Korean lo-fi study" and you'll find hours of content.

**Soft piano jazz.** Especially when I'm writing or reviewing grammar. Nothing too upbeat, nothing too dramatic. Solo piano works really well.

**Café ambience.** The sounds of a coffee shop — soft background conversations, cups clinking, rain. There are apps entirely dedicated to this (Coffitivity, A Soft Murmur). They create the feeling of being in a Korean café without leaving home.

**Drama OSTs.** Instrumentals from Korean drama soundtracks. They're great because they're melodic without being distracting. The Reply 1988 OST is my personal favorite for studying.

## My Study Ritual

About 30 minutes before studying, I make something warm — roasted barley tea (mugicha) if I have it, or any gentle herbal tea. I put on the playlist, open my notebook, and start without my phone nearby.

The first session is 25 minutes using the Pomodoro technique, followed by a 5-minute break. After three cycles, I take a 20-minute rest.

It doesn't always work perfectly. Some days I'm already on Instagram after 10 minutes. But having the ritual makes it easier to come back.

## The Most Important Thing

Consistency over perfection. A real 20-minute study session is worth more than a 2-hour one where half the time you're on your phone.

And if you can't study one day, it's okay. You start again tomorrow.
